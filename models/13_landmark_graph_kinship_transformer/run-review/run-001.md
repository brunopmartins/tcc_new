# Run 001 — M13 LGKT-Net minimal landmark graph

**Date:** 2026-05-26
**GPU:** AMD Radeon RX 6750 XT (gfx1031, ROCm 5.7)
**Status:** Manually stopped during ep 10 training (after ep 9 summary 0.8839). Peak Val AUC 0.8850 at epoch 5; best.pt from that epoch.
**Outcome:** **M13 R001 matches M02 R031 (Test AUC 0.8462 — literally identical to M02 R031 CV mean) and falls clearly below the M12 family** (R006 0.8788, R010 0.8754, R011 0.8825 single-run). Per EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md's stop criterion ("graph structure adds cost without improving AUC, low-FAR, or per-relation balance"), **the Model 13 line is stopped** after R001. The architecture works — AUC is 0.10 above M12 R001 (frozen baseline) — but the landmark-graph representation does not produce a meaningful advantage over either M02 R031 (CV-validated) or M12 R006-R011 (best M12 single-runs).

The result has one qualitatively interesting finding: **M13 R001 reaches the project's highest non-kin specificity (87.36 %)**, well above M12 R011 (78.69 %) and M02 R031. But the price is severe grandparent-class collapse (gmgs 17 %, gfgs 39 %), recall down to 60.4 %, and an inferior aggregate AUC. The trade-off is not favorable for the FIW task as evaluated.

---

## Context

Model 13 (LGKT-Net) was a proposed architectural successor to M12. The
hypothesis was that an **anatomical graph** built from landmark-derived
ROI tokens would represent kinship cues more faithfully than M12's fixed
crops. R001 was the minimal-architecture baseline:

- AdaFace IR-101 backbone (Phase 2 unfreeze of body[46:49] + output_layer)
- One backbone pass per face (vs M12's 5 crop-and-pass)
- ROIAlign over 8 canonical-landmark-derived component boxes
- 2-layer edge-aware Graph Transformer on the joint pair graph
- Native symmetric pooling
- BCE classifier head, no auxiliaries

Per EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md success threshold for R001:

> - Test AUC >= M12 R001 (0.7464)
> - no catastrophic regression on grandparent classes
> - training stable without direction-specific symmetric-forward loss

The first and third criteria pass; the second does not.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Training stability and speed.** ~8.6 min/epoch vs M12's ~26 min —
  the single-backbone-pass design delivers the predicted 3× speedup.
  No instability, no symmetric-forward trick needed (the symmetric
  pooler is order-invariant by construction).

- **Non-kin specificity 87.4 %.** Highest in the project. The graph
  representation does seem to filter out visually similar but
  unrelated pairs more aggressively than M12.

- **Order-invariance.** Confirmed by construction: the
  [mean, |diff|, prod] pooling cannot produce different scores for
  (A, B) vs (B, A). M12 R006's symmetric-forward trick is not needed
  here.

- **Threshold robustness.** Val-selected threshold 0.100, the
  lowest of any model in the project — the model's score
  distribution for kin pairs is compressed downward, but rankings are
  preserved.

### What failed

- **Test ROC AUC = 0.8462.** Matches M02 R031 CV mean *exactly*. -0.029
  vs M12 R010 single, -0.036 vs M12 R011. The graph representation
  does not produce a ranking advantage at this scale and recipe.

- **Catastrophic per-class collapse on small kin classes.** gmgs
  17.4 %, gfgs 38.8 %, gmgd 39.8 %, gfgd 50.0 %. M12 R011 holds these
  at 62.8 %, 79.6 %, 64.2 %, 74.6 % respectively.

- **Test Recall = 60.37 %.** -20+ pp vs M12 R010-R011. The model is
  too conservative for the FIW task.

- **Test F1 = 0.6935.** -0.10 vs M12 family. The precision gain does
  not compensate for the recall drop.

- **Val→Test gap = -0.039.** Worse than M12 R006-R011 family (gap
  -0.02 to -0.03). The graph model overfits slightly more than M12's
  cross-region attention.

### Why this happens

A few plausible reasons the landmark graph underperforms here:

1. **Spatial information loss in ROIAlign.** The 7×7 feature map at
   stage 4 has 16-pixel grid cells. Most landmark boxes are smaller
   than 16 pixels in the original 112-pixel space, so ROIAlign
   samples 1-2 grid cells per region. The "anatomical detail" is
   already smoothed away by the time we ROI-pool.

2. **No comparison-only ablation.** M12 R009/R010 showed that
   identity-as-feature in gA/gB is a real shortcut. M13 R001 includes
   the global node in its representation, so the same identity
   leakage may be present without the cmp-only counterweight.

3. **Graph transformer over-smooths small-class features.**
   Cross-face attention with global edges mixes information across
   classes. For rare classes (gmgs at 121 test pairs), the model has
   little signal to anchor, and the broad attention dilutes it
   further.

4. **Fixed canonical landmarks don't add value over fixed boxes.**
   Both M12 and M13 ultimately use fixed pixel-space regions —
   M12's boxes are larger and asymmetric; M13's are smaller and
   landmark-centred. The "landmark" framing was supposed to add
   adaptive geometry, but with pre-aligned FIW the landmarks are
   fixed by construction. The architectural value reduces to "smaller
   boxes + graph attention", which we now see is worse than M12.

### Per EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md decision rules

The plan specifies:

> Treat changes as inconclusive when:
> - Test AUC gain is below 0.008 and not replicated;
> - low-FAR gains come with major non-kin or grandparent collapse;

R001's TAR@FAR series are below M12 R011 across the board. Non-kin
specificity is higher, but **grandparent collapse is severe**, which
the plan explicitly calls out as a stop condition.

> Stop the Model 13 line if:
> - R001-R003 cannot beat M12 R001/R002-style numbers;
> - graph structure adds cost without improving AUC, low-FAR, or
>   per-relation balance;
> - landmark fallback rate is high enough to make the architecture
>   effectively a fixed-box model again.

The first two stop conditions are met. The third is moot — there is
no landmark detector at runtime, so the canonical-landmark design *is*
already a fixed-box model, just with smaller boxes. **Stopping is the
plan-aligned decision.**

### Implications

- **Single-pass ROIAlign over a 7×7 feature map is too coarse for
  fine-grained anatomical reasoning.** A potential remediation
  (stage-3 14×14 map, EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md R004) might unlock more
  capability, but the EV is now low given that even R001's coarse
  variant matches M02 R031 exactly — the spatial-detail argument
  predicts a smaller gain than the +0.04 needed to match M12.

- **The M12 family beats M13 R001 by an architectural margin.** The
  RGCK-Net combination of (1) explicit per-region AdaFace embeddings,
  (2) cross-region attention with direction-specific weights tamed
  by symmetric forward, and (3) comparison-only fusion produces a
  qualitatively different operating-curve profile that M13 does not
  reach with a graph transformer.

- **Project resources are better spent on**:
  1. **5-fold CV of M12 R011** to band the +0.0086 single-run gain
     (highest-EV remaining experiment).
  2. **Post-hoc low-FAR calibrator** (R011 score + B0 cosine +
     regional features) — uses already-trained models.
  3. **TCC narrative work** — the M12 R011 single-run result is
     already TCC-grade.

---

## Decision: Stop M13

Per EXPERIMENT_PLAN.md's "Stop the Model 13 line if" criteria, both
conditions 1 (cannot beat M12-style numbers) and 2 (graph structure
adds cost without improving AUC/low-FAR/per-relation balance) are
met. M13 is paused.

The implementation remains in the tree (model.py, AMD/train.py,
AMD/test.py, AMD/run_pipeline.sh) for future researchers who may
want to try a stage-3 variant or different landmark sources. R002+
runs are not planned.
