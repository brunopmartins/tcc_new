## Context

R001 is the first run of M12, implementing Phase 1 of the proposal
`proposta_rgck_net_kinship.md` (sections 37-38). The motivation comes
from a series of experiments on M09/M10/M11 where *sophisticating the
training distribution* over the M09 R001 full-FT AdaFace stack
(balanced sampling in M09 R002, hard negatives in M11 v4) consistently
raised Val AUC but **lowered** Test AUC. The pattern was clear: full-FT
AdaFace memorizes families seen during training (val pool shares
families with train), so any intervention that "trains harder" on the
val pool distribution exacerbates the generalization gap.

M12 tests a different direction: **what if we reduce AdaFace's
influence instead of training harder?** The architectural changes:

1. **Backbone frozen** — only 5.6 M / 70.7 M params train (7.9 %)
2. **Region tokens** — 5 anatomical regions extracted via fixed bounding
   boxes (rather than 49 or 196 patch tokens)
3. **Lightweight cross-attention** — 5×5 attention vs 49² or 196² in
   M09/M10/M11
4. **Regional gating** — sigmoid weights per region, interpretable
5. **BCE classifier head** — same as M09 R001's working setup

Expected outcome: Val AUC ceiling lower than M09 R001 (because frozen
backbone = less capacity) but Test AUC closer to Val AUC (because
frozen backbone = less family memorization).

Actual outcome: **directionally right, absolutely below M09 R001.** The
val→test gap is indeed the smallest of any AdaFace-based model
(-0.089), but the Val AUC ceiling (0.8351) is too low — Test AUC ends
at 0.7464, below M09 R001's 0.7982.

**Different optimization shape from full-FT models.** M09/M10/M11 had:
- Slow warmup drift (0.71-0.72 Val AUC for ep 1-4)

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Val→test gap is the smallest in the AdaFace family (-0.089).**
  Architectural hypothesis directionally validated. Reducing AdaFace's
  fine-tunable footprint *does* reduce family memorization.
- **Per-class balance at peak is unprecedented.** All 11 classes in
  75-92 % range at ep 3 val. The sibling classes that historically
  struggle (ss, bb, sibs) are all > 76 % — `ss` at 85.3 % in ep 1 was
  the strongest single-epoch siblings result in the project.
- **Time per epoch is ~half** of full-FT models (20 min vs 40 min) due
  to no backward pass through frozen backbone.
- **The Phase 1 baseline is well-behaved.** No instability, no
  oscillation crisis, no all-kin collapse. Clean training.

### What failed

- **Absolute Test AUC is below M09 R001 by -0.052** (0.7464 vs 0.7982).
  The smaller gap doesn't compensate for the lower Val AUC ceiling.
- **`fd` regressed catastrophically (-11.6 pp)** despite the more
  balanced per-class peak. The reason is unclear from the val phase
  alone — the per-region weights may not be appropriately learning
  cross-generational fd-specific cues.
- **Grandparent classes mostly stayed at M09 R001 level or below.** The
  hypothesis was that better per-class balance would lift grandparent
  generalization; instead `gfgd`, `gfgs`, `gmgs` are all below M09
  R001, only `gmgd` improved (+4.9).

### Hypothesis test result

**"Reducing AdaFace influence (frozen backbone) closes the val→test
gap while maintaining competitive Test AUC" — PARTIALLY confirmed.**

- Closes the gap: ✓ (-0.089 vs M09 R001 -0.094)
- Maintains competitive Test AUC: ✗ (0.7464 vs 0.7982)

The Phase 1 architecture is **directionally correct but quantitatively
underpowered**. The fix should be increasing capacity (Phase 2
unfreeze) without re-introducing the family memorization that hurts
full-FT models. Whether that balance is achievable is the next
experiment.

### Likely root cause of the lower ceiling

Two factors:

1. **5.6 M trainable params is too few** to learn the cross-generational
   patterns specifically. M09 R001 had 70.9 M trainable, M02 R031 had
   86 M. The MLP head, cross-region adapter, and gate together can only
   adapt to the kinship signal up to a point.

2. **5 region tokens may be too few or too coarse.** The fixed
   bounding boxes are anatomical but rigid. Slight misalignment
   between faces (the FIW alignment isn't perfect) could cause the
   region crops to be inconsistent across pairs. Phase 2 of the
   proposal (`Versão 2 — recorte por landmarks`) addresses this.

3. **The frozen AdaFace embeddings already cluster by identity.**
   Frozen means we can't move them. The cross-region adapter and gate
   are working *on top* of an identity-clustered space, trying to
   extract kinship signal from regional comparisons. This is harder
   than fine-tuning the backbone to push embeddings into a
   kinship-aware space.

## Next Step

The proposal's experimental sequence (§38) is the natural next set
of runs:

- **M12 R002 — Phase 2: partial unfreeze.** Unfreeze the last IR-101
  block (stage 4, 7×7×512). Should lift the Val AUC ceiling while
  preserving most of the gap benefit. **Highest priority next run.**
- **M12 R003 — Phase 3: full fine-tune.** Probably reintroduces family
  memorization. Lower priority.
- **M12 R004 — Phase 4: supervised contrastive auxiliary loss** (λ=0.05).
- **M12 R005 — Phase 5: relation-type auxiliary head.**
- **M12 R006 — Phase 6: hard negatives via `relation_matched`.** M11
  v4 already showed this hurts on full-FT — unknown if it helps on
  the frozen-backbone setup.

Of these, R002 is the only one with a clear hypothesis ("higher
capacity will lift the ceiling enough to surpass M09 R001"). The
others are exploratory.
