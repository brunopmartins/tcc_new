## Context

R002 became the project headline (Test AUC 0.8564) via Phase 2 partial
unfreeze (stage 4 + output_layer). R003 (Phase 4 SupCon λ=0.05) failed
to improve — Test AUC -0.005. R004 implements **Phase 6 of the
proposal §38 — hard negatives via `relation_matched`** — as the next
candidate intervention.

The `relation_matched` strategy constructs negatives by drawing the
partner of a positive `(role_A, role_B)` pair from another pair of the
*same relation type* in a different family. For example, a `father-son`
negative is constructed from a father and a son of two different
unrelated families. The negatives are visually kinship-like (right
role pairing, similar age structure) so the model has to discriminate
on *kinship-specific* cues, not on role/age cues.

The hypothesis tested: **partial-FT (stages 1-3 frozen) might be
sufficiently regularised that hard negatives don't widen the val→test
gap** the way they did on M11 v4 (full-FT). If the AdaFace identity
features in stages 1-3 stay intact, the model can't memorise
fold-specific patterns even with hard negatives — the only adaptive
parameters are stage 4 + output_layer + the cross-region head, all of
which see the entire training family pool.

Expected outcome: Val AUC up by 0.005-0.01 (hard negs help
discrimination), Test AUC neutral or slightly up if partial-FT does
prevent overfit.

**Actual outcome: Val AUC up by +0.003 (0.9323 → 0.9354, the project-wide
max), but Test AUC DOWN by -0.009 (0.8564 → 0.8473) and gap widened
from -0.076 to -0.088.** Per-relation: all 11 kin classes regressed by
1-11 pp. The "hard negs raise Val, drop Test" pattern from M11 v4 is
**reproduced exactly** in this partial-FT setup.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Val AUC reached a new project-wide maximum (0.9354)**, +0.003 over
  R002. Hard negatives DO improve the model's discrimination on the val
  pool. The training mechanism is producing better val-pool scores.
- **R004 stayed above R002 in every training epoch**. The improvement
  is not an early-epoch artefact — it's consistent through the entire
  trajectory.
- **Per-class val accuracies at peak were balanced** (84-97 % range,
  similar to R002 peak). The training is functioning correctly.

### What failed

- **Test AUC -0.009**. The Val AUC gain doesn't transfer.
- **Val→test gap widened** from -0.076 (R002) to -0.088 (R004).
- **All 11 kin classes regressed in test** by 1-11 pp. Non-kin improved
  marginally (+1.2 pp). The model shifted the decision boundary toward
  non-kin globally.
- **TAR@FAR=0.001 regressed by -2.2 pp** — at the strict-verification
  end, R004 is much worse than R002.

### Hypothesis test result

**"Partial-FT (stages 1-3 frozen) protects against the M11 v4 hard-
negatives pathology" — REJECTED.**

The pathology reproduces in M12 R004. Hard negatives:
- Raise Val AUC by ~+0.003 (similar magnitude to M11 v4's +0.007 vs M09)
- Drop Test AUC by ~-0.009 (similar magnitude to M11 v4's -0.028 vs M09)
- Widen val→test gap by ~+0.012 (similar magnitude to M11 v4's +0.034)

The effects in M12 R004 are slightly smaller in absolute magnitude
than in M11 v4 — possibly because the frozen stages 1-3 prevent the
deepest layer of overfit. But the *direction* is identical, and the
gap-widening signature is the same.

### Why this happens (cross-confirmed mechanism)

Both M11 v4 and M12 R004 show the same effect: `relation_matched`
negatives, by construction, draw partners from *training families*
that play the same role as the positive partner. This creates a
training signal that emphasises **discriminating within the training
family pool** — exactly the val-pool distribution.

Held-out test families have:
- Different specific identities (none of them in train)
- Same relation labels (same role pairings exist)
- Different photographic distributions (different cameras, ages, etc.)

The training pushes the model to focus on within-pool family separation,
which transfers poorly to held-out families. Random negatives, by
contrast, give the model practice rejecting *any* visually-plausible
non-kin pair, which transfers better.

### Implication for future runs

- **Hard negatives via `relation_matched` are now a CLOSED direction** —
  two independent experiments (M11 v4, M12 R004) have shown they hurt
  Test AUC. Further variations of this strategy are unlikely to be
  productive.
- **Two consecutive proposal-phase attempts have failed** (R003 SupCon,
  R004 hard negs). The Phase 4-6 sequence didn't add value to R002's
  stack on this dataset.
- R002 (Test AUC 0.8564) remains the project headline.

### What's left to try on M12

- **Phase 5 — relation-type auxiliary head**: still untried. Could help
  grandparent classes specifically by giving the model a relation-
  category signal. Requires dataset modification to pass relation int
  indexes to the loss (~moderate code change).
- **Architecture: ROI Align region tokenizer** (proposal §15
  Strategy 1): replace the 5-forward-pass tokenizer with a single
  feature-map ROI pool. Halves training time, may add small Test AUC.
- **Hyperparameter sweep around R002**: vary LR (5e-6, 2e-5), dropout
  (0.1, 0.3), gate hidden dim. Low expected EV.
