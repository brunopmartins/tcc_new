# Run 010 — AMD ROCm / RX 6750 XT / FIW / R006 + differential LR + comparison-only fusion

**Date:** 2026-05-17
**GPU:** AMD Radeon RX 6750 XT (gfx1031, ROCm 5.7)
**Status:** Manually stopped during epoch 10 training (after ep 9 summary at 0.8996). Peak Val AUC 0.9052 was reached at epoch 5 and tied at epoch 8 (the saved best.pt is from epoch 5).
**Outcome:** **Aggregate AUC essentially tied with R006 (-0.003 within noise floor); STRICT-FAR essentially tied with R009 (-0.22 pp); but R010 is the new MID-FAR champion (TAR@FAR=0.01 = 21.16 %, +2.55 pp vs R006) and has the highest Test AP of any M12 run (0.8567).** Test ROC AUC 0.8754 — third place on aggregate AUC after R006 (0.8788) and R002 (0.8564), but R010 dominates the verification operating curve in the mid-stringent range. Strict-FAR gain didn't compound to 6-7 % as hypothesised; instead the stack shifted the trade-off into a different operating regime than either parent. R010 is the most "balanced" run in the M12 cycle — top-3 on every Test metric.

## Context

R007 (differential LR) and R009 (comparison-only fusion) were the two
post-R006 interventions that moved strict-FAR metrics in the same
direction (R007 TAR@FAR=0.001 = 4.49 %, +1.16 pp vs R006 3.33 %; R009
TAR@FAR=0.001 = 5.43 %, +2.10 pp). Both were neutral on aggregate
Test AUC (within the ±0.009 noise floor measured in R004). The cycle
summary listed stacking them as the highest-EV remaining single-shot
run in the M12 cycle:

> "**R010 candidate — Stack R007 + R009** (differential LR + comparison-only fusion).
> Both moved strict-FAR in the same direction (R007: +1.16 pp, R009: +2.10 pp vs R006).
> Combining them might compound (strict-FAR could reach 6-7 %) while staying near
> R006's aggregate AUC."
> — `run-review/overview.md` line 394 (post-R009 update)

The hypothesis tested in R010:

> Differential LR (head at 4× the backbone LR) and comparison-only
> fusion (drop gA, gB from classifier input) target different
> mechanisms — the first lets the head adapt faster, the second
> removes identity-leakage signal from the classifier. Stacking them
> should compound the strict-FAR gains: target TAR@FAR=0.001 in the
> 6-7 % range while keeping aggregate Test AUC within the noise floor
> of R006 (0.8788 ± 0.009).

**Outcome: hypothesis PARTIALLY CONFIRMED.** Strict-FAR did not reach the 6-7 % target (R010 5.21 % ≈ R009 5.43 %, no compounding); but the stack produced the strongest mid-FAR results in the M12 cycle (TAR@FAR=0.01 = 21.16 %, the M12 best) and the highest Test AP (0.8567, M12 best). Aggregate Test AUC 0.8754 lies between R006 (0.8788) and R007 (0.8730) — within the ±0.009 noise floor of the headline. The two interventions interact non-additively: they share the same operating-curve-reshaping mechanism, so stacking them shifts the optimal operating point further than either alone, but the strict-FAR tail saturates near the R009 ceiling.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **TAR@FAR=0.01 = 21.16 %** ⭐⭐ — **the new M12 best**, +2.55 pp
  vs R006 (18.61 %), +1.57 pp vs R007 (19.59 %), +3.37 pp vs R009
  (17.79 %). For the standard mid-stringent verification operating
  point (1 false-accept per 100 trials), R010 is the strongest model
  in the project.

- **TAR@FAR=0.1 = 60.00 %** ⭐ — also the M12 best, +0.07 pp vs
  R006 (59.93 %). The 0.07-pp edge is within noise of any single
  run, but combined with the mid-FAR and AP wins it shows a
  consistent operating-curve lift.

- **Test AP = 0.8567** ⭐ — the highest Test AP of any M12 run, +0.001
  vs R006 (0.8561), +0.007 vs R009. Average Precision is the most
  appropriate threshold-invariant ranking metric for verification on
  imbalanced kinship sets, so this is a genuine quality signal.

- **TAR@FAR=0.001 = 5.21 %** — essentially tied with R009 (5.43 %)
  within typical strict-FAR noise. R010 is the *second*-best
  strict-FAR M12 run, much better than R006 (3.33 %) or R007 (4.49 %).

- **Precision = 75.43 %** — best of any post-R002 M12 run. Combined
  with the high non-kin specificity (74.8 %), R010 is the
  highest-precision M12 model.

- **gfgs gain held (+7.2 pp vs R006).** Even with the higher
  threshold cutting off other kin classes, the gfgs improvement
  from R009's comparison-only fusion transferred to the stack.

### What failed

- **Aggregate Test AUC -0.003 vs R006** — within the ±0.009 noise
  floor but R010 is not the new headline AUC model. R006 still wins
  on aggregate Test AUC summary.

- **Strict-FAR did not compound.** Hypothesis was 6-7 % at
  TAR@FAR=0.001 (R007 4.49 + R009 5.43 had suggested possible
  additive stacking). Actual: 5.21 % ≈ R009 alone. The two
  interventions converged at the same strict-FAR ceiling rather than
  adding.

- **Per-class regression on most kin classes** (2-14 pp vs R006).
  bb, ss, sibs, fs, fd, ms, md, gfgd, gmgd, gmgs all lost 2-14 pp.
  This is almost entirely the threshold shift (0.250 → 0.500).

- **gmgs collapse -14.1 pp** — the worst single-class regression in
  the M12 cycle. The grandmother-grandson class is now at 66.1 %.

- **Test F1 -0.007 vs R006** — the high-threshold operating point
  costs F1 enough to fall behind R006 in this composite metric.

| Strict-FAR (TAR@FAR=0.001) compounds to 6-7 % | 5.21 %, no compounding | **Falsified** |

But the stack revealed something the cycle summary didn't predict:
**R010 is the mid-FAR champion** (TAR@FAR=0.01 = 21.16 %, +2.55 pp
vs R006) **and the AP champion** (0.8567). These were not hypothesised
gains; they are unexpected wins. The stack didn't compound strict-FAR
but did compound *operating-curve quality* in the mid-stringent
region.

### Why this happens (mechanism)

The cycle summary's mechanism taxonomy already separated:
- Aggregate-AUC interventions (R006 symmetric forward) — shortcut removal
- Strict-FAR interventions (R009 comparison-only) — identity-leakage removal
- Capacity reallocation (R007 diff-LR) — neutral on aggregate

R007 alone gained +1.16 pp at TAR@FAR=0.001 (small) and +1.0 pp at
TAR@FAR=0.01 (small). R009 alone gained +2.10 pp at FAR=0.001 (large)
but only -0.82 pp at FAR=0.01 (negative).

R010 gains:
- TAR@FAR=0.001: +1.88 pp vs R006 ≈ R009's gain (5.21 vs R009 5.43)
- TAR@FAR=0.01: +2.55 pp vs R006 — **larger than the sum of
  R007's +0.98 pp and R009's -0.82 pp** alone (which would predict
  +0.16 pp); much larger than either.

The mid-FAR gain is therefore **superadditive** in R010 — combining
diff-LR + cmp-only produced a TAR@FAR=0.01 improvement that neither
intervention alone could achieve. Why:

1. **R009 hurt TAR@FAR=0.01** (-0.82 pp) because removing gA/gB
   shifted probability mass to the strict-FAR end. The mid-FAR
   region lost discriminative power.
2. **R007 helped TAR@FAR=0.01** (+0.98 pp) because the head LR
   ramped up faster and the head was learning the mid-FAR features
   more thoroughly.
3. **In R010, R007's mid-FAR boost compensates for R009's mid-FAR
   loss** — the diff-LR helps the smaller (cmp-only) classifier learn
   to discriminate in the mid-FAR region, recovering and exceeding
   what R009 alone lost.

The strict-FAR end remains R009-dominated (tail ceiling). The mid-FAR
region is jointly shaped by both interventions.

### What the threshold shift signals

R010's val-selected threshold is 0.500 vs R006's 0.250. The model
genuinely produces a different score distribution — narrower
positive-class scores, wider negative-class scores. This is
consistent with the comparison-only fusion making score outputs more
discriminative per pair, and diff-LR refining the classifier to
exploit this.

The cost: per-class accuracy regresses at the higher threshold because
some borderline positive pairs (especially in numerically dominant
classes like bb, fs, ms) fall below 0.500. The benefit: the score
distribution as a whole has a cleaner operating curve in the
mid-FAR region.

### Implications for future runs

- **R006 stays headline on aggregate Test AUC.** R010 is third
  (after R002 0.8564 — wait, R002 is 0.8564, so R010 0.8754 is
  ahead of R002 too: R006 > R010 > R009 > R007 > R002).
- **R010 becomes the new TAR@FAR=0.01 champion.** When the project
  discusses mid-stringent verification, R010 should be cited.
- **R010 also becomes the new TAR@FAR=0.1 champion** (60.00 %).
- **R010 becomes the new Test AP champion** (0.8567).
- **R009 stays the TAR@FAR=0.001 champion** (5.43 % vs R010 5.21 %).
- **Future work**: the mid-FAR superadditivity is a new phenomenon
  the cycle summary did not anticipate. If continuing M12 R&D,
  worth understanding whether other intervention pairs show
  similar non-linear compounding (most plausibly: a 4th
  intervention that adds to R007+R009 in the same direction).
  But the cycle summary's "TCC narrative > more M12 runs"
  recommendation still holds — these are operating-point
  refinements, not fundamental quality jumps.
