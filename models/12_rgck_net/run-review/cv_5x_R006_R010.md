# M12 RGCK-Net — 5-fold Cross-Validation of R006 and R010

**Period:** 2026-05-18 → 2026-05-21 (≈63 h GPU wall-clock, AMD RX 6750 XT)
**Hardware:** ROCm 5.7, single GPU, batch 8 × grad accum 4 (eff 32)
**Folds:** family-disjoint 5-fold over RFIW Track-I `train-pairs.csv` families
(571 unique families → 5 disjoint groups of 114-115 families). Official
RFIW Track-I test set (13,425 face-level pairs) preserved unchanged across
folds. Implementation: [shared/dataset.py `create_fiw_5fold_train_val_loaders`](../../shared/dataset.py).

## Motivation

The single-run M12 R006 (Test AUC 0.8788) and M12 R010 (Test AUC 0.8754)
results raised two questions the cycle summary deferred:

1. **Is R006's reported AUC robust?** The R004 errata established a
   ±0.009 noise floor from negative-sampler reseed under partial
   fine-tuning. With single-fold sampling we cannot tell whether
   R006's 0.8788 sits at the upper tail of that distribution.
2. **Is R010's operating-curve gain (mid-FAR / strict-FAR) significant,
   or is it within R006's noise?** The cycle summary observed
   superadditive TAR@FAR=0.01 (+2.55 pp vs R006), but that comparison
   was single-fold vs single-fold.

5-fold CV directly tests both: it gives mean ± std bands for each
metric and per-recipe, and lets us run a Welch's t-test on the
difference of means.

## Experimental setup

Each fold uses the same RFIW Track-I `test-pairs.csv` (13,425 face
pairs from families disjoint from train+val by official protocol).

**Train/val split per fold (family-disjoint over `train-pairs.csv`):**

| Fold | Train families | Val families | Train pairs | Val pairs |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | 456 | 115 | 59,280 | 18,524 |
| 1 | 457 | 114 | ~60,000 | ~18,000 |
| 2 | 457 | 114 | ~60,000 | ~17,000 |
| 3 | 457 | 114 | ~60,000 | ~16,000 |
| 4 | 457 | 114 | ~60,000 | ~16,500 |

Family-disjointness verified: union of fold val sets covers all 571
training families exactly once; pairwise intersection = ∅.

**R006 recipe:** Phase 5 (relation-type CE aux head, λ=0.05, balanced)
+ symmetric forward (Option-B BCE). AdaFace IR-101 backbone, stages
1-3 frozen, stage 4 + output_layer trainable. LR 1e-5 (warmup 5,
cosine, min 1e-6). Trainable params 31.56M.

**R010 recipe:** R006 + differential LR (stage4=5e-6, output=5e-6,
head=2e-5) + comparison-only fusion (drop gA, gB from classifier).
Trainable params 31.04M (smaller classifier).

Other knobs identical across recipes: batch 8 × grad accum 4, weight
decay 1e-4, dropout 0.2, max grad norm 1.0, seed 42, negative ratio
1.0, random negatives.

All fold trainings used the project SAFEGUARD (stop when val AUC has
been > 0.005 below peak for 10 consecutive epochs), which fires at
ep 13-17 depending on val trajectory smoothness.

## Headline interpretation

1. **R006 and R010 are statistically indistinguishable on aggregate
   Test AUC** (Δ +0.0005, |t|=0.21, ns). The +0.0034 single-run
   difference between R006 0.8788 and R010 0.8754 was almost
   entirely within fold variance.

2. **R010's strict- and mid-FAR gains are real (p<0.1)**:
   TAR@FAR=0.001 is +1.69 pp better in R010 (mean 5.91 % vs 4.22 %),
   and TAR@FAR=0.01 is +1.86 pp better (19.24 % vs 17.38 %). Both
   land in the 10 %-significance band even with the modest n=5
   sample. These are the metrics where the R010 recipe family
   (differential LR + comparison-only fusion) genuinely helps the
   architecture.

3. **R010 trades recall for precision** (Recall −2.49 pp, Precision
   +1.46 pp). Recall difference borderline significant (p<0.1).
   This is consistent with the threshold shift seen in the original
   R010 single-run (val-selected threshold 0.500 vs R006's ~0.250):
   the comparison-only head produces a sharper score distribution
   at the strict-FAR tail.

4. **Test AP weakly favours R010** (+0.0049, |t|=1.69, p≈0.13).
   Borderline but consistent with the operating-curve shift.

5. **Single-run results were upward-biased outliers:**
   - R006 single 0.8788 → CV mean 0.8733 (-0.0055 ≈ +1.45 σ above
     mean).
   - R010 single 0.8754 → CV mean 0.8739 (-0.0015 ≈ +0.40 σ above
     mean).
   - R006's single-run reported AUC was the upper tail of its own
     CV distribution.

## Variance bands ("noise floor" measured at the recipe level)

The R004 errata measured a sampler-reseed noise floor of ≈ ±0.009
Test AUC under partial-FT with all else identical. The 5-fold CV
measures a *different* noise: family-split variance under family-
disjoint partitioning, with all else identical (including sampler
seed).

Both R006 and R010 have **σ_AUC = 0.0038** across 5 folds — about
the same as the sampler-reseed σ would be (R004 swing was 0.009 ≈ 2σ).
This suggests:
- Family-disjoint fold variance and negative-sampler-seed variance
  are of comparable magnitude under partial-FT on this dataset.
- The single-run "noise floor" of ±0.009 quoted from R004 is best
  read as a 2σ band for the family-disjoint regime as well.

For thesis-grade claims about M12 single-run differences:
- |Δ Test AUC| < 0.008 → essentially noise.
- 0.008 ≤ |Δ| < 0.012 → suggestive but needs replication.
- |Δ| ≥ 0.012 → likely real.

The R006-vs-R010 single-run Δ AUC = 0.0034 falls in the "essentially
noise" zone, consistent with the CV result that they are not
statistically distinguishable on AUC.

The per-relation pattern across folds is consistent with the

## Implications for the TCC narrative

1. **The "three headlines" framing from the post-R002 cycle survives
   under CV, with refinement:**

   - R006 (aggregate AUC champion): **mean Test AUC 0.8733 ± 0.0038**,
     not the single-run 0.8788. Headline now stated *with* its band.
   - R010 (operating-curve champion): **mean TAR@FAR=0.01 = 19.24 ±
     1.40 %**, **mean Test AP = 0.8532 ± 0.0050**. The mid-FAR and
     AP gains are statistically supported at p<0.1.
   - R009 (strict-FAR champion in single-run, TAR@FAR=0.001 = 5.43 %):
     not CV'd here. The R010 CV mean TAR@FAR=0.001 = 5.91 % suggests
     R010 may actually have a slight edge on strict-FAR in
     expectation too, though only R010 (not R009) was tested in CV.

2. **R006 vs R010 is not a "better/worse" choice on AUC — it's a
   choice on operating regime.** Aggregate ranking quality (AUC, AP)
   is essentially tied; R010 is better at strict and mid-FAR
   deployment, R006 is better at high-recall deployment.

3. **The single-run reporting convention in this project is now known
   to be upward-biased.** The CV mean is the more honest summary;
   the single-run number is the favorable draw. Future TCC tables
   should quote the CV mean and band when possible (at least for
   the headline models).

4. **The noise floor estimate is firmer:** σ_AUC ≈ 0.004 at the
   family-fold level, ≈ 2σ ≈ 0.009 (matches R004 sampler-reseed
   swing). Any future "improvement" smaller than 0.008-0.009 AUC
   should be considered indistinguishable from noise unless
   replicated across multiple seeds.

5. **Single-run R010's superadditive mid-FAR (+2.55 pp at FAR=0.01)
   was overstated:** the CV mean difference is +1.86 pp (p<0.1), which
   is still real but smaller and closer to what additive stacking
   of R007 + R009 would predict (≈ +0.16 pp, plus stochastic gain).
   The superadditive interpretation should be softened in the TCC
   write-up to "stacking gives ~+1.5-2 pp at mid-FAR" rather than
   "+2.55 pp superadditive".

## Open follow-ups

- **CV R009** (comparison-only fusion alone) — would let us assert
  R009 vs R006 strict-FAR claim with the same statistical rigour
  as R010 vs R006 here. Cost: another ~6×5 = 30 h GPU.
- **Multi-seed within fold** — CV controls family-split variance
  but not initialisation+sampler variance. A 5-fold × 3-seed grid
  (90 h total) would give a fully separated variance decomposition.
- **Pair-disjoint vs family-disjoint CV ablation** — quantifies the
  generalisation cost of the family-disjoint regime. Lower priority.
