## Context

The first M12 R&D cycle (R005-R010) exhausted the structural intervention
family on M12 R002. The remaining open hypothesis from
[`cycle_R005-R010_summary.md`](cycle_R005-R010_summary.md) was:

> **Fix the `relation_matched` sampler** and re-run with actual hard
> negatives. The only intervention that has genuinely never been tested
> under a correct implementation.

R011 closes that gap. The intervention pairs two pieces:

1. **Sampler fix** (commit `fc3301d`):
   `_sample_fiw_rfiw_relation_matched_negatives` now tracks role pools
   per relation type during positive collection. For an "fs" hard negative
   it samples a p1-role face (father-like) from family A and a p2-role
   face (son-like) from family B ≠ A. The pair is visually plausible as
   an "fs" pair but the two people are not related.

2. **Curriculum** (`hard_negative_ratio=0.30`):
   30% of training negatives are role-matched, 70% are uniformly random.
   This matches the user proposal of "25-35% hard + 65-75% random":
   too much hard pressure is known from M09/M11 to widen the val→test
   gap and force memorisation; too little leaves the architecture
   shortcut-trained on easy negatives.

R011 = **R010 stack + role-matched hard negatives at 30% mix**.
Everything else identical to R010.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Test ROC AUC = 0.8825**: **NEW PROJECT HEADLINE.** +0.0086 vs R010
  CV mean, +1.45σ outside the family-fold noise floor. The first M12
  Run-to-Run intervention to move aggregate AUC outside the noise band
  since R006.

- **Test AP = 0.8645**: also the project champion. +0.0113 vs R010 CV
  mean, +2.26σ. AP is more sensitive to the kinship-positive class
  ranking than AUC, so this confirms the score distribution itself
  improved.

- **TAR@FAR=0.001 = 7.51 %**: **NEW PROJECT CHAMPION.** Above the
  AdaFace B0 frozen baseline (7.06 %) and the prior best M12 R009
  (5.43 % single, 5.91 % R010 CV mean). At strict verification
  thresholds, M12 R011 is now the strongest trained model in the
  project, full stop.

- **TAR@FAR=0.01 = 21.30 %** and **TAR@FAR=0.1 = 61.89 %**:
  M12 family bests. The operating-curve gains hold across the full FAR range.

- **Test Precision = 77.77 %**: best M12 by a clean margin (+2.34 pp
  vs R010 single, +4.52 pp vs R010 CV mean). Combined with the recall
  drop, R011 is operating at a substantially more conservative
  decision boundary while still ranking better overall.

- **Non-kin specificity +3.9 pp**: the design goal of "improving
  non-kin rejection" is achieved. R011 is meaningfully better at
  saying "no" to visually plausible but unrelated pairs — exactly
  what the role-matched hard negatives should produce.

- **Val→Test gap = -0.021**: smallest gap of any R0xx in M12. Training
  on harder negatives reduced the generalisation gap, not widened it
  (the M09/M11 failure mode of "harder train hurts" did not materialise
  here, because the 30% mix kept the model from over-specialising).

### What failed

- **Test F1 = 0.7938**: -0.002 vs R010, -0.008 vs R006 — within noise
  but R006/R010 still win this composite. F1 punishes recall drops
  more than precision gains.

- **Test Recall = 81.05 %**: -3 pp vs R010 single, -6 pp vs R010 CV
  mean. Borderline-significant against R010 CV (t≈2.9). This is the
  cost of the precision gain: a more conservative threshold misses
  some borderline positives. For deployment scenarios that need high
  recall, R006 is still preferable.

- **Grandparent kin classes regress 3-7 pp.** gmgd in particular
  drops -7.3 pp. The strict threshold + sharper score distribution
  hurts small classes more.

### Why this works (mechanism)

The previous shortcut taxonomy (R006-R010) identified three operating
regimes that interventions affect:

| Intervention type | Targets | Example | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shortcut removal — direction | aggregate AUC | R006 symmetric forward | removes `attn_ab` direction-specific bias |
| Shortcut removal — identity | strict-FAR tail | R009 cmp-only fusion | removes gA/gB identity leakage |
| Capacity reallocation | mid-FAR | R007 diff-LR | trains head harder |
| **Distribution hardening** | **operating curve + AUC + specificity** | **R011 role-matched hard negs** | **closes the "easy negative" gap** |

R011 introduces a **fourth class of intervention** — improving the
*training data distribution* rather than the model. The previous
classes are all model-side changes. This one is data-side.

The role-matched hard negatives are pairs that look visually
plausible as kin but aren't. Before R011, the model only saw uniformly
random cross-family negatives (typically very different ages, genders,
or general facial structure — easy). It learned to discriminate kin
from "anyone different". After R011, it sees 30% pairs that *look*
like kin but aren't — same role distribution as the positives. To
discriminate these, the model has to learn finer-grained features.

The result is a model with:
- Higher AUC (better overall ranking)
- Higher AP (better positive-class ranking specifically)
- Higher TAR@every FAR (better at low-FAR operating points)
- Better non-kin specificity (the most direct effect)
- Slightly lower recall (the cost of the sharper threshold)

The intervention is **complementary** to R006/R009 — not competing.
R006 fixed direction shortcuts, R009 fixed identity leakage, and now
R011 closes the easy-negative gap. All three can stack.

### Why grandparent classes regress

gmgd and gmgs are the smallest classes (123 and 121 pairs in test).
The hard negatives include grandmother-grandchild-like pairs that
are not actually related, which the model now correctly rejects more
often. But the threshold shift to 0.300 also makes some legitimate
grandparent positives fall below threshold — the classes with the
highest within-class score variance (typically the smallest classes)
are hit hardest.

This is the same pattern seen in R010 vs R006 (recall trade-off);
R011 amplifies it because hard negatives compress the negative score
distribution upward, forcing the threshold lower.

### Implications for future runs

R011 is the new headline. Future work options:

1. **CV R011** (5-fold, ~30 h) — confirm the +0.0086 AUC gain is not
   a favorable single-fold draw. Highest-EV next step.
2. **Vary `HARD_NEGATIVE_RATIO`** at 0.20, 0.40, 0.50 to find the
   curve's optimum.
3. **Curriculum** as originally proposed (0 % → 30 % over 3 epochs)
   to see if early-warmup easier negatives produce a better solution.
4. **Stack with M12 R011 + lower relation_aux λ** (R012 candidate) —
   the proposal mentioned recovering some precision via reduced aux
   weight. With R011 already winning on precision, this may be lower EV.
5. **Pivot to writing.** R011's results are clearly TCC-grade — the
   single-run number is well outside the noise floor of the previous
   project headline. Single CV confirmation could be enough.
