# Run 008 — AMD ROCm / RX 6750 XT / FIW / R006 + L2-SP (λ=1e-3)

**Date:** 2026-05-16
**GPU:** AMD Radeon RX 6750 XT (gfx1031, ROCm 5.7)
**Status:** SAFEGUARD-stopped at epoch 16 (Val below peak 0.9052 for 10 consecutive epochs).
**Outcome:** **Numerically tied with R006 on every threshold-invariant Test metric.** Test ROC AUC **0.8788** (vs R006 0.8788), Val→test gap **-0.026** (vs R006 -0.026). L2-SP at λ=1e-3 was empirically a no-op on Test discrimination. Train loss did rise substantially (R008 ep 7: 0.344 vs R006: 0.260) — confirming the penalty was being applied and constraining the backbone — but the final solution converges to the same predictions on the held-out test set.

---

## Context

R006 (Test AUC 0.8788) is the project headline after symmetric forward
closed the val→test gap to -0.026. R007 (differential LR) was neutral
on Test (-0.006 within noise floor). The next priority in the overview
was R008: L2-SP regularisation on top of R006.

L2-SP anchors the trainable backbone params to their AdaFace pretrained
values via a squared-distance penalty:

  L_total = L_R006 + λ_l2sp · Σ_i ‖θ_i − θ_i^(0)‖²

where θ_i^(0) are the AdaFace weights at the start of training. The
hypothesis tested:

> With the unfrozen backbone (stage 4 + output_layer, 25.9M params)
> tethered to AdaFace pretrain, the head adapts to kinship while the
> backbone retains identity-discriminative features. We expect either
> a further val→test gap closure (and a small Test AUC lift) or a
> roughly neutral result — depending on whether R006's symmetric
> forward had already exhausted the gap-closure available.

**Outcome: empirically neutral on Test.** L2-SP at λ=1e-3 did constrain
the backbone (train loss visibly higher) but the val→test gap stayed
at -0.026 and Test AUC at 0.8788 — both numerically identical to R006
at 4-decimal precision. Per-relation accuracies are within ±2 pp of
R006 in every class.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **The penalty was correctly applied.** Train loss was 0.084 higher
  than R006 at ep 7 — the backbone was being held closer to AdaFace
  pretrain. The mechanism is verified.
- **Val AUC trajectory was slightly more stable** (post-peak decline
  was 0.003-0.005 gentler than R006). This is the expected effect of
  a regulariser.
- **R008 confirms R006's solution is stable** under additional
  regularisation. The model isn't relying on backbone drift that
  L2-SP would constrain away.

### What failed

- **Test AUC change 0.0000** vs R006 (within numerical precision of
  reporting). L2-SP at λ=1e-3 did not move the threshold-invariant
  Test metric.
- **Test AP +0.0002** — also within noise.
- **TAR@FAR=0.001 -0.55 pp**, **FAR=0.1 -0.28 pp** — small, within
  per-class noise.
- **Per-class changes ≤0.4 pp** in 10 of 11 kin classes — at this
  scale, indistinguishable from sampling noise.

### Hypothesis test result

**NOT CONFIRMED (the lift hypothesis); CONFIRMED (the neutral
hypothesis).** The intervention was empirically neutral on Test. Two
interpretations:

1. **λ=1e-3 is too small.** The penalty has visible training-loss
   effect but not enough to change Val/Test distributions. A higher
   λ (1e-2 or 1e-1) might produce a different outcome — either
   helping or hurting. Untested.

2. **R006's symmetric forward had already exhausted the gap-closure
   available from this regularisation family.** The val→test gap of
   -0.026 may be close to the irreducible test-set noise for this
   model class on FIW, given the family-disjoint protocol. Adding
   another generalisation regulariser on top doesn't help because
   there's nothing left to regularise.

The two interpretations are not mutually exclusive. A future R008b
with λ=1e-2 could distinguish them: if Test AUC rises, hypothesis 1
holds; if it drops or stays flat, hypothesis 2 dominates.

### Why this happens (mechanism)

L2-SP is conceptually similar to R006's symmetric forward in that
both are generalisation regularisers — they constrain the solution
space to reduce over-fitting on the training distribution. R006
constrains the model to be order-invariant; L2-SP constrains the
trainable backbone to stay close to a known-good starting point.

The post-R002 R&D cycle now suggests that for this dataset + model
combination, R006's symmetric forward captures most of the available
gap-closure. Subsequent regularisers (L2-SP here, comparison-only
fusion in R009, planned) compose either neutrally or with diminishing
returns.

This is consistent with the structural taxonomy emerging from
R005-R008:

| Run | Intervention type | Effect on Test AUC |
|---|---|---|
| R005 | per-class loss | -0.009 (within noise, class balance improves) |
| R006 | structural symmetry | **+0.022** (decisive) |
| R007 | capacity reallocation | -0.006 (within noise) |
| R008 | parameter anchoring | **+0.000** (neutral) |

R006's intervention is qualitatively different — it removes a
class of *shortcut* from the architecture. The others adjust the
training dynamics within the same shortcut-containing architecture.
R006 is the gap-closing move; R008's L2-SP doesn't add a second
gap-closing dimension.

### Implications for future runs

- **R008 confirms R006 is a robust solution.** Adding L2-SP regulariser
  doesn't break it (Val/Test both stable) but doesn't help either.
- **R006 stays headline.** R008 is a tie, not an improvement.
- **R009 (comparison-only fusion)** is the next planned run. It's a
  *structural* intervention (removes identity-as-feature from the
  classifier) — closer in spirit to R006's symmetry than to R007/R008.
  Higher probability of moving Test AUC than R007 or R008.
- **Two paths if R009 also fails to move Test:**
  - **R010 candidate: L2-SP λ=1e-2** to distinguish the two
    interpretations above. Low-EV if R009 has the same neutral
    signature.
  - **Conclude that R006 is near-optimal** on the threshold-invariant
    metrics for this dataset/architecture, and shift focus to other
    operating-point and per-class refinements (e.g., R007-style
    diff-LR specifically tuned for strict-FAR).
