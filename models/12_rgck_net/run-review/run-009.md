## Context

R006 (Test AUC 0.8788) is the project headline. R007 (differential LR)
and R008 (L2-SP λ=1e-3) were both neutral on Test AUC. The structural
taxonomy emerging from R005-R008 suggested that *shortcut-removal*
interventions (like R006's symmetric forward) are the only family
that has moved Test AUC. R009 tests the last remaining intervention
in that family.

The intervention: drop `gA` and `gB` (the contextualised global tokens
for face A and face B) from the classifier input at
[model.py:374](../model.py#L374). The default fusion concatenates
`[gA(512), gB(512), |diff|(512), prod(512), sims(K), weights(K),
regional_score(1)]` (2049 dims). R009 fusion drops the first two
slots: `[|diff|(512), prod(512), sims(K), weights(K),
regional_score(1)]` (1035 dims).

The hypothesis tested:

> `gA` and `gB` carry identity-as-feature signal. Even with R006's
> symmetric forward removing direction-specific shortcuts, the
> classifier can still learn family-identity patterns from the raw
> global tokens. Removing them forces the head to discriminate on
> explicit comparison features only (difference, product, regional
> similarities, regional score). Expected: Val AUC drops (less raw
> signal); Test AUC may rise if the identity leakage was a
> meaningful memorisation pathway.

**Outcome: Val AUC predicted drop confirmed (-0.004 vs R006), Test
AUC essentially unchanged (-0.005 within noise).** But the operating
characteristics shifted substantially: TAR@FAR=0.001 +2.1 pp (the
biggest strict-FAR jump in the M12 R&D cycle), per-class kin
accuracies +2-9 pp on most classes (especially gfgs +9.2 pp),
non-kin specificity -4.4 pp.

R009 is doing something — it's reshaping the score distribution to
better separate kin pairs at the extreme tail — but the aggregate
AUC summary doesn't capture the win because the operating-point
shift redistributes probability mass.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Val AUC dropped by exactly the predicted amount.** -0.004 peak,
  consistent with "less raw signal in the classifier input" → harder
  to fit Val. The mechanism is verified.

- **TAR@FAR=0.001 jumped +2.1 pp vs R006 (5.43 % vs 3.33 %).** This
  is the biggest strict-FAR gain in the entire post-R002 R&D cycle.
  At the strictest verification threshold (1 false-accept in 1000
  trials), R009 is the strongest M12 model.

- **9 of 11 kin classes improved 1-9 pp** vs R006. Especially gfgs
  (+9.2 pp) — the grandfather-grandson class that was hardest at
  R006 (76.5 %) is now at 85.7 %.

- **Val→Test gap stayed essentially tied with R006** (-0.027 vs
  -0.026). The identity-leakage removal didn't destabilise
  generalisation — it just shifted *what* signal the model uses.

- **No re-introduction of family memorisation.** Train loss falls
  faster (smaller classifier fits faster) but Val/Test stay close
  together. The gap remains the second-smallest in the M12 family.

### What failed

- **Aggregate Test AUC -0.005 vs R006** — within the ±0.009 noise
  floor. R009 doesn't beat R006 on the headline summary metric.
  Test AP -0.006, similar magnitude.

- **TAR@FAR=0.1 -1.3 pp**, **TAR@FAR=0.01 -0.8 pp** — the
  trade-off for the strict-FAR gain is mid-range FAR. R006 stays
  better at the more permissive operating points.

- **Non-kin specificity -4.4 pp**, **Precision -2.3 pp**. The
  operating-point shift towards higher recall has costs.

- **gmgs regressed -2.5 pp**. Three of four grandparent classes
  gained (gfgd +1.5, gfgs +9.2, gmgd +3.2); only gmgs dropped.

### Hypothesis test result

**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED.** The prediction was "Val AUC drops, Test
AUC may rise." Val dropped as expected (-0.004), Test AUC was
neutral (-0.005 within noise). But the *operating curve* shifted
in a useful way: strict-FAR much better, mid-FAR slightly worse.

The interpretation: gA and gB *were* carrying some signal in R006.
Removing them changed the per-class behaviour substantially (most
kin classes up, especially gfgs). But the signal they carried was
distributed across the AUC curve in a way that the comparison
features can partially recover at the extreme tail (strict-FAR
gain) but lose at mid-range. The net AUC summary is approximately
the same.

The R009 model is "more decisive" — sharper at the extreme
thresholds. The R006 model is "more averaged" — better mid-range
discrimination.

### Why this happens (mechanism)

`gA` and `gB` are the contextualised AdaFace embeddings of the two
faces. They carry identity information about who each person is.
The classifier head was using this signal in two ways:
1. As a comparison helper — the head can implicitly compute
   `gA - gB` or `gA · gB` from the raw vectors.
2. As an identity prior — given gA looks like "an older man" and
   gB looks like "a young child", the head can use that to bias
   the kinship prediction.

Use #1 is healthy and is now explicitly provided via `|diff|` and
`prod`. Use #2 was the suspected leakage — the head learning
"these specific demographic profiles tend to be kin in the training
distribution." Removing the raw gA, gB blocks Use #2.

The R009 evidence suggests Use #2 was a real but small effect:
- If it had been a huge effect, removing it would have moved Test
  AUC substantially. It didn't.
- If it had been zero, removing it would have produced no change
  anywhere. But the per-class accuracies shifted by 2-9 pp.
- The strict-FAR gain (+2.1 pp at FAR=0.001) is consistent with
  having removed a signal that was a "shortcut to identification"
  — at low FAR the model now relies more cleanly on the explicit
  comparison features, which are more transferable.

### Implications for future runs

- **R006 stays headline** on aggregate Test AUC.
- **R009 is the new strict-FAR champion** for M12 trained models:
  TAR@FAR=0.001 = 5.43 % is +1.25 pp over R002 (the prior best
  among trained models at 4.18 %). Useful for any deployment where
  the false-accept cost is asymmetric.
- **The shortcut-removal taxonomy is now exhausted.** R006 +
  R009 are the two interventions in this family; the rest of the
  M12 R&D cycle has been evaluated. Further AUC gains will likely
  require either:
  - A different intervention family (e.g., R010 candidate: L2-SP
    at higher λ; or non-architectural moves like better data,
    different backbone, or ensembling).
  - An architectural change of larger scope (e.g., ROI Align
    tokenizer for a different feature space).
- **R007 + R009 stack** is now interesting — both moved strict-FAR
  in the same direction (R007: +1.2 pp, R009: +2.1 pp vs R006).
  Combining them might compound (strict-FAR could reach 6-7 %)
  while staying near R006's aggregate AUC.
