## Context

R001 (Phase 1, fully-frozen backbone) achieved the smallest val→test
gap of any AdaFace-based model in the project (-0.089) but Val AUC
peaked at only 0.8351, giving Test AUC 0.7464 — below M09 R001's
0.7982. The Phase 1 architecture was directionally validated but
the frozen backbone capped the ceiling too low.

R002 implements **Phase 2 of the proposal** §38 (`destravar último
bloco`): unfreeze the deepest IR-101 block (body[46:49] — stage 4 with
3 BasicBlockIR units at 7×7×512) plus the output_layer (the FC head
producing the 512-d embedding). This adds 26 M trainable params on
top of R001's 5.6 M, bringing the total to **31.55 M trainable
(44.6 % of total)**. Stages 1-3 (~40 M params) stay frozen.

The hypothesis: with stage 4 + output_layer trainable, the model can
adapt the deepest face-discriminative features to be kinship-aware
while stages 1-3 keep AdaFace's robust low-/mid-level representations
intact. LR is dropped to 1e-5 (per proposal §37, backbone-unfrozen
should use 1e-5 vs head-only 1e-4) to prevent the unfrozen stage 4 from
melting.

Expected outcome: lift Val AUC ceiling to ~0.86-0.88; if gap stays
near R001's -0.089, Test AUC could reach 0.77-0.79.

**Actual outcome: dramatically better than expected.** Val AUC peaks at
**0.9323** (ep 4) — the highest in the entire project. Test AUC =
**0.8564**, surpassing M02 R031's prior project headline (0.850) by
**+0.006**. Val→test gap is -0.076 — larger than R001's -0.089 but
smaller than M09 R001's -0.094, and the higher Val AUC ceiling more
than compensates.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Phase 2 partial unfreeze is the right capacity-vs-overfit tradeoff.**
  Phase 1 (R001) was capped too low (Val AUC 0.835). Full FT (M09
  R001) reaches Val 0.892 but val→test gap widens to -0.094. M12 R002
  hits Val 0.932 with gap -0.076 — both metrics simultaneously better.
- **LR 1e-5 was correct.** With stage 4 trainable, a higher LR risked
  melting the backbone's deepest features (which AdaFace pretrained for
  identity discrimination). 1e-5 lets stage 4 adapt slowly while the
  cross-region head learns more aggressively at the same effective LR.
- **The per-region anatomical structure adds real information.** M09
  R001 used 196 patch tokens and 49 patch tokens (multistage). M12
  uses 5 anatomical tokens. Despite ~40× fewer attention slots, M12
  R002 captures kinship signal more effectively. The structured prior
  (eyes, nose, mouth) gives the model a useful inductive bias.
- **Convergence was fast.** Peak at ep 4. Total wall time for training
  ~180 min vs ~280 min for M09 R001.

### What failed

- Nothing critical failed. The result exceeds expectations.
- Train loss continued to descend after Val AUC peaked (typical
  overfit signal). The model would have continued to overfit if not
  stopped. Manual stop at 3 epochs of decline was the right call.
- The val→test gap (-0.076) is larger than R001's (-0.089). The
  trade-off is that R001 had Val AUC ceiling too low, while R002 has
  more capacity but slightly more memorization. **The R002 trade-off
  is the better one** in absolute Test AUC.

### Hypothesis test result

**"Partial unfreeze of stage 4 + output_layer lifts the Val AUC
ceiling while preserving most of the gap benefit" — CONFIRMED.**

- Val AUC ceiling lifted: ✓ (0.9323 vs R001 0.8351, +0.097)
- Test AUC improved: ✓ (0.8564 vs R001 0.7464, +0.110)
- Val→test gap stayed reasonable: ✓ (-0.076 vs full-FT M09 R001 -0.094,
  M10 R003 -0.140, M11 v4 -0.128)

### Why it works

Three factors interact:

1. **Frozen stages 1-3** preserve AdaFace's low- and mid-level
   identity-discriminative features. These features are robust and
   transfer well to held-out families (low memorization risk).
2. **Trainable stage 4 + output_layer** lets the model adapt the
   deepest face representation (the embedding itself) to be kinship-aware
   rather than purely identity-aware. This is the "fine-tune the last
   block" insight from face recognition literature applied here.
3. **Region tokens + cross-region attention + gating** provide an
   inductive bias that's structurally aligned with how kinship
   manifests (regional traits — eye shape, jaw structure). The model
   doesn't have to learn *where* to look from scratch.

The result is a model that's **both more discriminative on val** and
**generalizes better on test** than any prior project model.

## Next Step

R002 is the new project headline. Future directions per the proposal:

- **R003 — Phase 4: supervised contrastive auxiliary loss** (λ=0.05 per
  proposal §28). Possibly lifts Val AUC further; main risk is widening
  val→test gap.
- **R004 — Phase 5: relation-type auxiliary head.** Could improve
  grandparent classes specifically.
- **R005 — Phase 6: hard negatives via `relation_matched`.** M11 v4
  showed this hurts on full FT; unknown how it behaves on M12's
  partial-FT setup.
- **R006 — Phase 3: full fine-tune.** Probably re-introduces
  memorization (gap would widen toward M11 v4's -0.128). Lower priority.
- **R007 (architectural) — Strategy 1 ROI Align on shared feature map.**
  Phase 1 of the proposal §15 recommends starting with Strategy 3
  (fixed partition) and progressing to ROI Align. Could halve training
  time further and potentially lift the ceiling slightly.

For now, R002 establishes the new project baseline. M02 R031's reign
as project headline (0.850 since the early ViT experiments) is over.
