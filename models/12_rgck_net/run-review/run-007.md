# Run 007 — AMD ROCm / RX 6750 XT / FIW / R006 + differential LR

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**GPU:** AMD Radeon RX 6750 XT (gfx1031, ROCm 5.7)
**Status:** SAFEGUARD-stopped at epoch 15 (Val AUC below peak 0.9093 for 10 consecutive epochs).
**Outcome:** **Approximately tied with R006 on the threshold-invariant metrics.** Peak Val AUC rose by +0.004 (0.9049 → 0.9093) as predicted, but Test ROC AUC slipped by 0.006 (0.8788 → 0.8730 — within R004's ±0.009 noise floor). Diff-LR partially recovered the strict-FAR performance R006 had sacrificed: TAR@FAR=0.001 +1.2 pp, TAR@FAR=0.01 +1.0 pp. R006 remains the project headline.

---

## Context

R006 (Test AUC 0.8788) is the project headline after symmetric forward
closed the val→test gap to -0.026. The next direction in the R006
overview was differential LR: split the trainable params into 3 groups
and let the head adapt faster while the backbone melts slower.

Today (R002-R006) the optimiser uses a single LR (1e-5 peak with
warmup + cosine). The hypothesis tested in R007:

> Splitting the trainable params into (backbone stage 4, backbone
> output_layer, head) and using a 4× higher LR for the head will let
> the head reach its capacity earlier in training while keeping the
> backbone from melting away the pretrained features. We expect a
> small Val AUC lift (the head can adapt more) and a similar or
> slightly improved Test AUC.

**Outcome: Val AUC predicted lift confirmed (+0.004 peak), Test AUC
no net change (within noise floor).** The diff-LR change behaved
qualitatively as predicted on Val but did not translate to a Test
gain. R006's symmetry remains the dominant intervention; diff-LR is
neutral on this dataset/architecture combination.

## Analysis

### What worked

- **Val AUC peak rose by +0.004** vs R006, consistent with the
  hypothesis: a 4× higher head LR (2e-5 vs uniform 1e-5) lets the
  head reach a marginally higher fit on val.
- **Strict-FAR recovery**: R007 wins TAR@FAR=0.001 by +1.2 pp and
  TAR@FAR=0.01 by +1.0 pp vs R006. Partially restores the strict-FAR
  advantage R002 had, which R006 had sacrificed to gain mid-range
  AUC.
- **Mostly tied on Test AUC** within the ±0.009 noise floor.
  Differential LR is empirically neutral on Test, not regressive.

### What failed

- **Test AUC -0.006 vs R006** — no net gain. The 4× head LR did not
  produce a Test improvement. The Val→Test gap widened from -0.026
  to -0.036 (still small, second-best in the AdaFace family).
- **TAR@FAR=0.1 -2.4 pp** vs R006 — at the operating point most
  comparable to deployment in retrieval / large-pool settings, R007
  is worse.
- **gmgs -5.0 pp**, **gfgd -2.1 pp** — the diff-LR shift hurt two of
  the four grandparent classes. The other two (gfgs +3.1 pp, gmgd
  +1.6 pp) improved. Net average grandparent acc: 79.3 % vs R006
  79.9 % (-0.6 pp).

### Hypothesis test result

**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED.** The mechanism worked as predicted on Val
(head reached higher ceiling), but did not translate to Test. The
intervention is approximately neutral on Test AUC, with a small
shift of the operating curve toward strict-FAR.

The post-R002 R&D cycle has now established:
- **R006's symmetric forward** = the decisive Test AUC mover (+0.022).
- **R005's relation aux head** = strong per-class help (especially
  combined with R006's symmetry; multiplicative).
- **R007's differential LR** = neutral on Test; minor strict-FAR
  recovery.

### Why this happens (mechanism, tentative)

R006's symmetric forward was a *structural regulariser*: it forced
the model to learn ordering-invariant features that transfer across
families. This is a generalisation-improving intervention.

R007's differential LR is a *capacity reallocation*: it lets the
head fit better and the backbone change less. This is a fit-quality
intervention — it changes *how well* each component learns the
training signal, but it doesn't change *what* signal the components
can learn.

On a dataset where the train→test gap is the main bottleneck (as
R006 demonstrated), R007's intervention misses the lever. The head
already fits well at 1e-5; making it fit better at 2e-5 doesn't help
on test because the model isn't bottlenecked on training-set fit.

### Implication for future runs

- **R006 remains the project headline** (Test AUC 0.8788). R007's
  -0.006 is within noise floor.
- **Differential LR can be kept on or turned off**: it's neutral on
  Test AUC and slightly shifts the strict-FAR operating point. For
  deployment in low-FAR scenarios, R007 may be preferable; for
  balanced discrimination, R006.
- **The next direction (R008: L2-SP)** is a different kind of
  regulariser — it pulls backbone weights toward their pretrained
  values, which is structural like R006's symmetry but more
  surgical. Expected to either help close the residual gap further
  or be roughly neutral.
- **R009 (comparison-only fusion)** removes the identity-as-feature
  signal that may still be a small contributor to memorisation. May
  hurt Val but help Test.
