## Analysis

### What worked

Without the SAM ensemble, everything ran smoothly: 28 min/epoch (vs 77
in R001), stable VRAM ~5 GB at batch=8, no NaN/inf, no instabilities in
the AMP path. The pipeline is sound; the optimisation isn't.

### What failed

**Cosine_contrastive + AdaFace IR-101 full fine-tune oscillates rather
than converges.** Train loss kept dropping monotonically (1.93 → 0.43)
but validation AUC bounced in a 0.13-wide band with no upward trend. The
F1-optimal threshold on val flipped between 0.1 and 0.9 across
consecutive epochs — *opposite* sides of the sigmoid centre — indicating
the model was producing scores in qualitatively different distributions
between epochs.

### Hypothesis test result

**M10's intended drop-in replacement under M02's recipe is rejected.**
Swapping the ViT for AdaFace IR-101 while keeping cosine_contrastive
loss and the same LR schedule produces an unstable optimisation surface,
not a strict improvement.

### Likely root cause

Two interlocking factors:

1. **Loss orientation ambiguity.** Cosine_contrastive pulls positives to
   similarity = 1 and pushes negatives below similarity = margin. It
   does *not* constrain absolute embedding direction. With a randomly
   initialised cross-attention head plus a backbone that already has
   strong structure (AdaFace identity clusters), the optimiser can land
   on equivalent low-loss solutions with opposite orientations between
   epochs.

2. **AdaFace's pretrained structure resists.** AdaFace was trained with
   additive-margin softmax (push *identities* apart). Family members are
   distinct identities in WebFace4M — the backbone has already learned
   to keep them well-separated. Fine-tuning with cosine_contrastive
   tries to pull them back together. At small effective batch (32) the
   gradient signal isn't large enough to dominate AdaFace's prior
   structure on every step, so the system oscillates.

M02's ImageNet ViT didn't have this conflict — ImageNet features have
no built-in opinion about identity. Cosine_contrastive was free to
shape the representation from scratch.

## Next Step

**R003: BCE classifier head.**

```bash
SKIP_INSTALL=1 \
ALIGNED_ROOT=/home/bruno/Desktop/tcc_new/datasets/FIW_aligned \
BATCH_SIZE=8 \
GRAD_ACCUM=4 \
USE_CLASSIFIER_HEAD=1 \
LOSS=bce \
NUM_WORKERS=4 \
SEED=42 \
bash models/10_adaface_facor/AMD/run_pipeline.sh
```

The classifier head (`AdaFaceFaCoRClassifier` in [model.py](../model.py))
consumes `[emb1, emb2, emb1-emb2, emb1*emb2]` → MLP → 1 logit, trained
with BCE. This **decouples the kinship decision from embedding
orientation**: the embeddings can keep AdaFace's identity-clustering
structure, while the classifier MLP learns to score pairs from their
relative features (the diff/product terms). The same head is the standard
ablation against cosine_contrastive in M02.

If R003 also fails to climb past Val AUC 0.75 we'll know the problem is
deeper than the loss (likely full-FT instability of AdaFace on the small
FIW dataset) and the next move is freezing the lower IR-101 stages.
