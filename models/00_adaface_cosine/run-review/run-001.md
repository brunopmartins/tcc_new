**Date:** 2026-05-14

**Status:** COMPLETED — single-pass evaluation, no training step

---

## Context

Implementation of **§5 of `baselines_rgck_net_tcc.md`** — the minimal
no-adaptation reference. The script loads AdaFace IR-101 (WebFace4M
weights), extracts L2-normalised 512-d embeddings for every face in
the FIW Track-I val/test pairs, computes cosine similarity per pair,
selects an F1-optimal threshold on validation, and reports test
metrics at that threshold.

**No training.** Pure inference + cosine + thresholded scoring. The
script runs once and produces the final numbers.

The hypothesis tested:

> What kinship verification performance is achievable from an off-the-shelf
> face recognition model with no kinship-specific adaptation?

If RGCK-Net substantially exceeds this baseline, that's direct evidence
the kinship task requires more than face identification. **Surprisingly,
B0 turns out to be highly competitive with several trained models** —
beating M09 R001's Test AUC and producing the highest TAR@FAR=0.001
of any model in the project.

so at any threshold that captures most kin pairs it also misclassifies

**Surprising findings:**

1. **B0 Test AUC 0.7991 > M09 R001's 0.7982** — the no-training
   baseline beats the best AdaFace-based full-FT model. The training
   gain is marginal at the AUC level.

2. **B0 has the highest TAR@FAR=0.001 of any model** (7.06 % vs
   M12 R002's 4.18 % and M02 R031's 2.5 %). At very low false-acceptance
   rates, raw AdaFace cosine is the strongest discriminator. This is
   because AdaFace was trained for *identity* discrimination — strict
   verification at low FAR is exactly what its training optimised for.

3. **B0 has the smallest val→test gap of any model** (-0.024). With
   no training, there's no family memorisation. This is the true
   "generalisation ceiling" for AdaFace embeddings on FIW.

4. **B0 trails M12 R002 by -0.057 on Test AUC.** Translating this:
   M12 R002's architectural contributions (region tokens, cross-region
   attention, partial unfreeze) lift Test AUC by about 7 % relative
   over a no-training baseline. That's a real but moderate gain.

## Analysis

### What this tells us about the project

The project has several models trained with substantial effort
(hours/days of GPU time) that score within ±0.05 Test AUC of a model
with zero training. Specifically:

- M09 R001 (Test 0.7982) is **0.001 below** B0 (Test 0.7991). All that
  training basically reproduced the zero-shot result.
- M09 R002 (balanced sampler, Test 0.7824) is **0.017 below** B0.
- M11 R001 v4 (hard negatives, Test 0.7707) is **0.029 below** B0.
- M10 R003 (top-only, Test 0.7478) is **0.051 below** B0.

These four trained models all under-perform the no-training baseline.

The models that **do** beat B0 by meaningful margins:
- M12 R002 (+0.057): the region-token architecture + partial unfreeze
- M02 R031 (+0.051): ViT-B/16 with full fine-tune

### Why this is consistent with the proposal narrative

`baselines_rgck_net_tcc.md` §5.5 explicitly states the B0 purpose:

> Esse baseline serve como referência mínima. Ele mostra o quanto um
> modelo facial pronto consegue fazer sem adaptação ao problema de
> parentesco. Se o RGCK-Net superar muito esse baseline, isso
> demonstra que o problema exige mais do que reconhecimento facial
> tradicional.

B0 confirms: **most of the kinship signal is already encoded in
AdaFace embeddings.** Family members share facial features, which
AdaFace (trained to discriminate identities) encodes naturally because
"share features" → "less dissimilar in embedding space" → moderate
cosine similarity.

What the trained models contribute on top of B0 is *calibration* of
when "moderate cosine similarity" means "kin" rather than "two random
people who happen to look alike". M12 R002's region-token architecture
does this calibration most effectively (+0.057 over B0).

### What this means for the TCC

This is exactly the comparison the proposal wanted. B0 establishes the
floor; M12 R002 (project headline) establishes the ceiling; the
+0.057 gap is the *measured value* of the proposed RGCK-Net
architecture. Honest scientific reporting requires noting that the
baseline is stronger than naive expectations would predict.
