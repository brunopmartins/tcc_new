# Model 02 — 5-fold Cross-Validation of R031

**Period:** 2026-05-22 → 2026-05-24 (≈48 h GPU wall-clock, AMD RX 6750 XT)
**Hardware:** ROCm 5.7, single GPU, batch 32 (no grad accum), full FT 100.7M params.
**Folds:** family-disjoint 5-fold over RFIW Track-I `train-pairs.csv` families
(571 unique families → 5 disjoint groups). Official RFIW Track-I test set
(13,425 face-level pairs) preserved unchanged across folds. Implementation
reuses the M12 splitter
[shared/dataset.py `create_fiw_5fold_train_val_loaders`](../../shared/dataset.py).

## Motivation

R031 (Test AUC 0.850 single-run) is the canonical Model 02 result used in
all comparison tables in the TCC. The M12 cross-validation
([m12 cv doc](../../12_rgck_net/run-review/cv_5x_R006_R010.md))
showed that R006's single-run reported 0.8788 was a +1.45σ favorable draw
relative to its CV mean of 0.8733 ± 0.0038. The same question applies to
R031: how robust is its 0.850 number?

5-fold CV under the same methodology (family-disjoint train+val, fixed
official RFIW Track-I test) gives a direct apples-to-apples comparison
with M12.

| Test Accuracy | 0.7440 | 0.7466 ± 0.0108 | -0.0026 | -0.25σ |

| Test AP | 0.8170 | 0.8131 ± 0.0045 | +0.0039 | +0.87σ |
| TAR @ FAR=0.001 | 0.0250 | 0.0219 ± 0.0063 | +0.0031 | +0.49σ |
| TAR @ FAR=0.01 | 0.1400 | 0.1349 ± 0.0119 | +0.0051 | +0.43σ |
| TAR @ FAR=0.1 | 0.4990 | 0.4964 ± 0.0255 | +0.0026 | +0.10σ |

**R031's single-run results were at the favorable side of the distribution
(+0.96σ on AUC, +0.87σ on AP), but well within plausible CV variance.** The
single-run number is not an outlier; it just happens to sit above the
expected value by roughly one standard deviation.

The σ_AUC = 0.0040 measured here matches the σ_AUC = 0.0038 from the M12
5-fold CV — both architectures have ~the same family-fold variance under
this dataset.

## Cross-model comparison (CV vs CV, same methodology)

| Model | Recipe | Test AUC CV mean ± std | Δ vs M02 R031 (σ) |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| **M02 R031** | ViT-B/16 full FT + FaCoR + contrastive | **0.8462 ± 0.0040** | — |
| **M12 R006** | AdaFace IR-101 partial FT + Phase 5 + sym fwd | **0.8733 ± 0.0038** | **+0.0271 (~6.8σ)** |
| **M12 R010** | R006 + diff-LR + cmp-only fusion | **0.8739 ± 0.0038** | **+0.0277 (~7.0σ)** |

**The M12 family's advantage over M02 R031 on Test AUC is statistically
massive** (~7σ) and stable across CV folds. Both M12 recipes consistently
outperform M02 R031, and the gap is far larger than fold-level variance.

This validates one of the central claims in the TCC narrative: the
M12 RGCK-Net architecture (AdaFace IR-101 + region tokens + cross-attention
+ symmetric forward) is genuinely better than M02 (ViT-B/16 full FT +
FaCoR) for FIW family-disjoint kinship verification, not just on a single
favorable draw.

Side-by-side TAR@FAR profile:

| Operating point | M02 R031 (CV) | M12 R006 (CV) | M12 R010 (CV) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| TAR @ FAR=0.001 | 2.19 % ± 0.63 % | 4.22 % ± 1.28 % | **5.91 % ± 1.22 %** |
| TAR @ FAR=0.01 | 13.49 % ± 1.19 % | 17.38 % ± 1.16 % | **19.24 % ± 1.40 %** |
| TAR @ FAR=0.1 | 49.64 % ± 2.55 % | 57.68 % ± 0.89 % | **58.92 % ± 1.46 %** |

M12 dominates M02 at every operating point. The most dramatic gap is at
strict verification (TAR@FAR=0.001): M12 R010 nearly triples M02 R031.

## Implementation notes

- Same `create_fiw_5fold_train_val_loaders` function used in M12 CV.
- M02 train.py was extended with `--fold` and `--num_folds` flags
  (commits `6973e5c`, `d333e86`); run_pipeline.sh added FOLD, NUM_FOLDS,
  RUN_OVERRIDE env vars.
- The R031 recipe required `LOSS=contrastive` (supervised contrastive,
  the loss used in R031), not the pipeline default `cosine_contrastive`.
  This was caught by the loss-scale mismatch in the first fold 0 attempt
  (train loss ~2.7 vs R031's ~0.008).
- Fold 2 was interrupted mid-training by a manual `rm -rf output/033`
  while the train process was still writing checkpoints. The best.pt
  (saved at ep 2 from the val-AUC peak 0.8472) was preserved on disk
  and tested manually with `--threshold 0.900` (the val-selected
  threshold) to produce the reported metrics. AUC/AP/TAR@FAR are
  threshold-invariant and identical to what a fresh fold 2 would have
  produced from the same best.pt; threshold-dependent metrics (Acc/F1)
  used the explicit threshold 0.900 to match other folds.

## Implications for the TCC narrative

1. **Quote M02 R031 as CV mean 0.8462 ± 0.0040** in comparison tables,
   not the single-run 0.850. The CV mean is the honest summary; the
   single-run is the favorable draw.

2. **M12 R006/R010 dominance over M02 R031 is statistically robust
   (~7σ).** This is the strongest cross-model claim in the project and
   should anchor the architectural story:
   - Region-token attention with proper backbone selection (AdaFace IR-101
     identity-aware embeddings) beats full ViT fine-tuning with FaCoR
     top-only by 2.7 pp Test AUC.
   - The same training data, same family-disjoint evaluation, same RFIW
     Track-I test — only architecture differs.

3. **σ_AUC ≈ 0.004 holds across recipes and architectures.** The noise
   floor estimate is consistent: both M02 and M12 show ~0.4 % Test AUC
   variance across family-disjoint folds. Any single-run "improvement"
   smaller than ~0.008 should be treated as within noise.

4. **R031's single-run TAR@FAR=0.001 = 2.5 %** was slightly above the
   CV mean (2.19 %) but the difference is within σ. The strict-FAR
   ranking R010 > R006 > R031 holds robustly:
   - M12 R010 CV: 5.91 %
   - M12 R006 CV: 4.22 %
   - M02 R031 CV: 2.19 %
